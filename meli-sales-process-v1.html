Section 0
Before every call — the truth
Read this before you get on Zoom. Every single time.
The reality check
Eva makes $4,000–$8,000 a month. She is not the ideal candidate. She doesn't fully exemplify the products. She doesn't do everything she should. And she still makes $4,000–$8,000 a month.

Evelyn makes $20,000 a month. She showed up. She talked to people. She was consistent for 7 years.

You made $13,900 last year — barely trying, in spurts, while working a smoke shop for $16 an hour.

The women DMing you already want what you have. They saw your life and reached out. Your only job on this call is to help them stop talking themselves out of it.
Sales is not pushing — here is what it actually is
Sales feels pushy when you try to convince someone of something they don't want. That's not this. These women chose to reach out after watching your life. They already want in. Your job is to lead them to a decision they already want to make. Staying silent at the close isn't being kind — it's abandoning them at the moment that matters most.
The one rule that fixes 90% of your calls
Ask one question. Then stop talking completely. Do not fill the silence. Do not add to the question. Do not say "mm-hmm" every five seconds. Wait until she finishes her full answer. The more she talks, the more she convinces herself. Every word you add is a word she didn't say about why she wants this.
The sale — keep this in your head the entire call
"The only wrong answer is being interested, watching other women live the life you know you could live — and doing nothing."
Be honest about what this is
This is not "make $10k in 90 days." This is a side income that grows into a full income with consistency. Most women build this alongside their current job for 6–18 months before it replaces it. That's the honest pitch — and it's more powerful than hype, because the Latina woman who's skeptical of MLM has already been burned by that promise. The truth closes more deals than the fantasy.

Section 3
Set the frame — take control
1–2 minutes. You set the agenda. Say this before anything else.
Why this step cannot be skipped
Without a frame, the call has no structure. The prospect thinks she's on an info call. Then when you try to close she feels surprised or pressured. The frame sets the expectation of a decision today — so when you get to the close it feels like the natural next step, not a sudden ask. You told her where you were going. She said yes. Now you can hold the structure.
Say this — word for word
"Okay so here's how I like to do these — I keep them short and focused, about 20–25 minutes, because I respect your time.

What I'm going to do is ask you a few questions to really understand where you're at and what you're looking for. Then I'll show you what we're doing and how women are building income and a real community with this. And if it feels like a fit, we'll figure out how to get you started.

Sound good?" (wait for yes — do not move forward without it)
Why "sound good?" matters
When she says yes to the frame, she has agreed to the process — including the decision at the end. You told her exactly what was happening. She said yes. This is not manipulation. This is leadership.

Section 7
The close
2–3 minutes. Best case / worst case. Assumptive close. Then complete silence.
The most important rule in the entire system
After you ask the close question — do not speak. Not one word. The first person who speaks loses. If she is quiet for 10, 20, 30 seconds — that is her thinking. Let her think. Your silence tells her this is a normal, confident moment. The second you fill that silence, you take the pressure off and she relaxes back into "I'll think about it."

Section 8
Objection handling
Never argue. Acknowledge — find the real issue — reframe — return to close.
The rule before every objection response
Objections are almost never what they sound like. "I don't have the money" usually means "I'm not sure yet." "I need to think about it" means "you haven't answered my real question." Your job is always to find the real question underneath — not respond to the surface one. Ask, don't argue. Then return to the close.
"I need to think about it"
Step 1 — Acknowledge: "Of course — I completely understand."
Step 2 — Find the real issue: "Can I ask — what specifically are you thinking about? Is it the $150, how the business works, or something else?" (pause — wait for her real answer)
If it's money: → go to money objection below
If it's doubt about herself: "That's honestly the most common thing I hear from the women who end up doing the best here. You don't need to have it figured out before you start. You just need to start — and I will be with you every step of the way."
If she still won't name it: "I hear you. Let me ask — is there anything I said that didn't feel right, or does this just feel like a big step?" (this almost always gets the real answer)
Return to close: "Based on everything you told me — [her words] — I think the only thing standing between you and that is this one decision. What's your gut telling you right now?"
"I don't have the money"
Step 1 — Acknowledge: "I hear you — and I want to be real with you, not dismiss that."
Step 2 — Clarify: "Can I ask honestly — is it that $150 genuinely isn't available right now, or is it more that you're not sure yet if it's worth it?" (pause — this separates the real money objection from a confidence objection)
If it's a confidence issue (most common): "Because that's a different conversation — and honestly the easier one. The product alone is worth $300. You're not spending $150, you're redirecting it. Worst case you have $300 in products you were going to buy anyway. That's a win before you even start."
If money is genuinely tight: "Okay — I respect that completely. When do you think would feel more right? Because I'd hate for you to miss this moment — especially with Ecuador launching in June. The women getting in now will be the leaders when it opens."
Return to close: "Do you have your card or should we figure out a timing that works for you?"

Section 9
Follow-up sequence
For prospects who didn't close on the call. Three touches. Then let go.
The mindset for follow-up
Most sales happen on the 2nd or 3rd contact, not the first. But the follow-up has to feel like genuine care — not desperation. You are not chasing her. You are checking in because you believe in what you showed her and you want to make sure she has what she needs to decide. She will feel the difference.
Touch 1 — Same day or next day
Send within 24 hours of the call. Warm, no pressure, opens a door.
"Hey [name] — it was so great connecting with you today. I just wanted to say I really enjoyed our conversation and I genuinely think you'd be an amazing fit. No pressure at all — whenever you're ready I'm here. And if any questions came up after we hung up, just send them my way 😊"
Touch 2 — 3–4 days later
Reference something real from the call. Reconnect to her reason.
"Hey [name]! Just thinking about you — you mentioned [her specific reason from discovery] and it really stuck with me. I wanted to check in and see if you had any questions or if anything else came up. Also just a reminder — Farmasi has new promotions activating this week, so it's a good time if you've been thinking about it. Either way, hope you're having a great week ✨"
Touch 3 — 7–10 days later
Final touch. Light urgency. Honest and warm. Then let go.
"Hey [name] — I don't want to keep bothering you, so this will be my last check-in for a while 😊 I just want you to know the door is always open. Whenever the timing feels right, I'm here. And if you ever want to revisit what we talked about, just send me a message. Wishing you the best — truly."
For Ecuador leads — add this to Touch 2 or Touch 3
"Also — I want to make sure you know that Farmasi is officially launching in Ecuador in June. Women who start building now are going to be the established leaders when that market opens. If timing has been the question, this is the reason to move now."
After 3 touches with no response — let go
Do not send a 4th message. Move on. Some people need more time and they will come back when they're ready. The ones who come back later almost always say "I wish I had started when we first talked." Your job is to stay visible through your content — not to chase individually.
The best follow-up is posting your life consistently so she keeps seeing what she's missing.

"Welcome to the team! 🎉 I'm so excited you're here. Here's what happens next — I'm going to send you three things: your login, a link to our team group, and a quick voice message walking you through your first steps. You don't need to figure anything out alone. I've got you." (send within the hour of her joining — speed here determines whether she stays excited or gets cold feet)
