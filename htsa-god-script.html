Reality check
They already want change — they need you to lead through fear with clarity. Your job is structure + confidence, not convincing.
Your edge
Authentic, #1 rep energy — add a tighter clock. Warmth lives in tone all call, not 20 minutes of small talk.
Overtalk leak
Ask one question → stop. Silence lets them sell themselves. Every word you add after the question is a word they didn’t say.
3-ask rule
Close up to 3 times — read the room. IRA / real logistics vs 26yo fear are different games.

Section 2 · 5–7 min
Frame the call
Conversational, not corporate. Let them feel: we’re figuring out fit together — not pitching blind.
Why
Without this they think it’s an info call. You’re naming fit both ways — and that you’ll still point them right if it’s a no.
"So what these calls are all about — really — is to see if high-ticket sales makes sense for you, and if you'd be a good fit to get support from our team."

"To do that I want to dig a bit more into your background and your goals, and then we can see together if this is the right career path for you."

"And if I don't think it's the right fit — I'll shoot you straight and help point you in a better direction. But if it is a fit, I'll walk you through exactly how we help people get started in high-ticket sales and what the next steps would be to get you moving forward — at the end of this call."
"Sound fair?" Stop. Let them answer.
if"Just tell me the price" / "How does it work?" early▶
"I'll walk you through everything — the investment makes way more sense once I understand you first. Literally a few minutes. Can I ask you a couple quick things?"
Section 3 · 7–27 min
Discovery
Order: one day (yesterday) → what they like → fork (double down vs acknowledge) → pain → money → optional 3.5 reframes → heaven / gap. They talk ~80%. Do not open 3.5 analogies before you’ve run likes + pain — those lines land after context.
Rule
One question → silence. A normal week invites a life story — use a single day (yesterday) instead. When you use an analogy from 3.5, tell it like a story, then stop.
3.1 Current reality — one day, tied in
Application lead
"I see on your application you're listed as a [Realtor / college student / insurance agent / D2D rep / investment adviser / W2 / etc.] — but I'd rather hear it from you."
Setter lead
"I heard from your call with Nick that you're a [Realtor / college student / insurance agent / etc.] — but I want it in your words."
"What do you do for work — for real? Here's what I mean: walk me through yesterday. Not your whole career story — just yesterday, start to finish, however you remember it."

"We're talking on a [say today's weekday] — so yesterday for you. Main beats are fine; I don't need every minute."

Mirror back in their words when they're done.
ifThey still drift into “my whole week” / life story▶
"I love the context — tighten it for me though. Pick one day last week, whatever's clearest — what did that day actually look like hour by hour or in main chunks?"
3.2 What they like — then the fork
"Okay — honestly that doesn't sound so bad. What's the one part of what you're doing that makes you the happiest or most fulfilled?" Silence.
ifThey seem to genuinely like the job / vibe is positive▶
"So you actually like [the piece they named] — help me out then: why not just double down on [their job or path] and get world-class at that?" (You're not attacking — you're surfacing the gap between liking a slice and being on this call.)
ifThey’re negative overall / already venting about the grind▶
"Oh, I got you — so you like [what they said is okay] but you're sick of [y and z — use their words]. That makes total sense why you'd be looking for something remote / different." → Then roll into 3.3 pain naturally.
3.3 Pain dig
Pain
"On the flip side — what do you not like about it that has you open to talking about something different?"
Then: "Give me a real example from the last thirty to sixty days where you felt that the most. What happened?" → "In that moment, what were you feeling?" → "What was the worst part for you?" → "How long has it felt like that — better, same, or worse?"
If they minimize pain — pick one (slow delivery)
"Let me ask it a different way — and I want you to actually think out loud with me. If nothing changes, what does this look like in two days… two weeks… two months… two years? Not for me — for you." (Say it slow. Let them answer each beat if you can.)

"Where does [their pain] go if we don't address it — does it shrink, or does it usually get heavier?"
"It's like brushing your teeth — skip one day, whatever. Skip a year, and the problem isn't 'I missed a day,' it's a whole different conversation. So I'm not being dramatic for fun — I want to know where this goes for you in six to twelve months if the pattern stays the same."
Tony line when it fits: "Tony Robbins says change happens when the pain of staying the same becomes greater than the pain of change — so what's the real cost if nothing changes in the next six months, for you personally, not just the money?" Then silence.
3.4 Money reality
"And right now, what are you actually bringing in from that — on average per month? Not your best month. The real average."

"If nothing changed and you stayed on this track — where do you realistically see that income in the next twelve months? Same ballpark, a little more, or pretty capped?"
If they have a income goal — direction vs effort
"Do the math with me for a second. If you want [their goal] and you're at [current] right now — how many more hours would you need at your current rate to even get close? Is that even possible without inventing new physics?" (Pause.)

"So if the math doesn't work there — the real question isn't whether you're willing to grind. It's whether the direction is broken. Fair?"

When to use
Open these after you know their day, what they like, pain, and money — not instead of 3.1–3.4. Pick the branch that matches their situation. You can also weave a line into the Story Bridge handoff: “What you said about [pain] reminds me of…”
ifCorporate / W2 / capped raises▶
Lead with the paradox — slow, casual:

"Can I ask you something that might sound weird? If you worked twice as hard next month… how much more money would you actually make?" (Silence.)

"So your effort goes up, but the reward barely moves — is that fair?"

If they dodge: "If you keep doing exactly what you're doing and everything goes right — where does this path lead in three to five years? Excited or capped?"

If tenure: "You've been there [X] years — how much has income actually moved vs cost of living?"
