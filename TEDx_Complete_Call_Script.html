🛡️ PHASE 4: OBJECTION HANDLING

🎯 Andres Objection Handling Method

Flow: ALWAYS start with Money → Time/Husband/Fear (max 4 reframes) → Apology

⚡ CRITICAL RULES:
  • ALWAYS default to MONEY first (even if they say "husband" or "time")
  • Maximum 4 reframes per objection → Then apologize and move on
  • After reclose: SHUT UP → Silence closes deals
  • Use 1-10 scale to isolate and validate objections

💰 PHASE 1: MONEY (Always Start Here)

Step 1: Money Aside (Isolate Value)

"Money Aside" Close

"Money aside, if I gave you $11,000 cash in a duffel bag right now, would you want to give that TEDx talk and get your message out to millions?"
→ LISTEN.

If YES: "So it's not about the VALUE of getting on that TEDx stage—it's purely the money, right?"

If NO: You haven't built enough pain/value. Go back to discovery.

Step 2: 1-10 Close (Isolate Belief)

1-10 Scale

"On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you that giving a TEDx talk would transform your business and authority?"
→ They give number (hopefully 8-10)

If 8-10: "Great! So you BELIEVE in the outcome. The only thing standing between you and that TEDx stage is the money, right?"

If below 8: "What would it take to get you to a 10?" → Address that concern first

Step 3: Best Case / Worst Case Close

Best Case / Worst Case

"Let me ask you this... The reason we're so successful—90% placement rate, Kevin Breel's 5M views, Eli Nash speaking for Fortune 500s—is because we follow a proven framework. So realistically..."
"BEST case: Your TEDx talk goes viral like Kevin's. 5M views, $100K book deal, 250+ speaking gigs. How would that change your life?"
→ Let them paint picture
"WORST case: You get 50,000 views (our guarantee), one consulting client worth $15K signs because they saw your TEDx talk. You've already made your investment back plus some. Does that make sense?"
→ Get micro-yes
"So even in the WORST case scenario, you win. And in the BEST case, you're looking at life-changing results. Knowing that, what's really holding you back?"
→ LISTEN for real objection

Step 4: Isolate the "Person" Threat

"So is this about the money itself, or is it about what someone ELSE might think about the investment?"
→ LISTEN. This reveals if it's REALLY money or if it's spouse/fear.

Step 5: Permission It Up

"So if you had the money available right now—like it was sitting in your bank account ready to go—would it make this decision easier?"
→ Get YES. You're setting up payment plan.

Step 6: Cash on Hand

"Fair question: What do you actually have available right now that you could put toward this?"
→ LISTEN. If they give a number, you have buying intent.

Step 7: Create Payment Plan

"Okay, here's what we can do to make this work..."
$4,500 today to secure your spot and start working with your TEDx coach immediately. Then $4,500 in 90 days—right around when your talk goes live and you start seeing results. That way you're spreading the investment but getting started NOW. Does that work for your budget?

Step 8: RECLOSE

"Okay [firstname], so this gets you everything we talked about—the coaching, the placement, the 50K view guarantee—and fits your budget with the payment plan. Let's lock in your spot. How would you like to proceed?"
→ SHUT UP. Wait for response. First to talk loses.

🔀 Possible Outcomes After Reclose

✅ Outcome 1: CLOSED SALE

"Perfect! Let me send you the invoice right now..."

❌ Outcome 2: They Give Another Objection

Common objections after money phase:

  • "I need to talk to my husband/wife" → Go to SPOUSE OBJECTION
  • "I don't have time" → Go to TIME OBJECTION
  • "What if my talk isn't good enough?" → Go to FEAR OBJECTION

👥 SPOUSE OBJECTION: "I Need to Talk to My Husband"

Most common TEDx objection - Handle with Andres framework

Step 1: Partner Aside (Isolate)

"I totally get that. Let me ask you this though: If your husband said YES, DO THIS TEDx talk right now, would you?"
→ LISTEN for "YES" or hesitation

If YES: Move to Heavy is the Head frame

If hesitation: "What would hold YOU back even if he said yes?" → There's another objection underneath

Step 2: Heavy is the Head Frame

"I understand—heavy is the head that wears the crown."
Obviously you respect him and you want his input. I totally get that. But here's what I've noticed...
"This is YOUR investment in YOUR authority. This is YOUR 18 minutes on that red dot. Who's giving the TEDx talk—you or him?"
→ She says "ME"
"Exactly. So whose responsibility is it to put YOU in the best position to share your message with millions?"
→ She says "MINE"

Step 3: Consequence

"Here's what I've seen happen... Every time someone says 'I need to talk to my husband,' they come back in 3 weeks and the spot's gone. And they regret not acting when they had the chance."
"Is that a risk you're willing to take with YOUR TEDx message?"
→ PAUSE

Step 4: Pushback → Reclose

Pushback: "I still really need to talk to him first..."
Reclose with Risk Reversal:
"Okay, here's what we can do. Let's hold your spot with the $4,500 deposit today. You talk to your husband tonight about the TEDx opportunity. If he says NO, we'll refund the deposit—no questions asked. But if he says YES, you're already locked in and working with your coach this week. Fair?"
"Because the question is: Is YOUR TEDx message worth $4,500 of risk to keep the spot?"
→ RECLOSE
⚠️ If still NO after 4 reframes: "I understand. I've tried everything I can to help you get on that TEDx stage. Unfortunately, your message is going to stay bottled up. I'm really sorry—I wish I could've helped." → End warmly, next prospect

⏰ TIME OBJECTION: "I Don't Have Time"

Step 1: Time Aside (Isolate)

"Time aside, if you DID have the time, would you want to give that TEDx talk and get your message out there?"
→ They say YES
"So it's not about WHETHER you should do this—it's just about WHEN, right?"

Step 2: Two Types of People Frame

"Let me share something I've noticed. There are two types of people..."
Type 1: "I'm too busy with work, kids, life—I CAN'T give a TEDx talk right now."
Type 2: "I'm busy with work, kids, life—which is exactly WHY I HAVE TO give a TEDx talk. Because once I do, everything else gets easier. The authority, the credibility, the opportunities—it all compounds."
"Which type are you?"
→ They say Type 2

Step 3: Consequence

"So let me ask you this: If I come back to you in 2 years and you STILL haven't given your TEDx talk because you were too busy, how would that feel?"
→ Let them sit in that
"Would you actually be okay with that? Your message staying inside you for another 2 years?"
→ They say NO

Step 4: Reclose

"Then let's not let that happen. 60-90 minutes a week for 12 weeks. Kevin Breel was 19 with no time. Susan Adams ran a corporate finance team. They made it work because they decided their message was a PRIORITY, not just an idea. Let's get you started this week."
→ RECLOSE
⚠️ If still NO after 4 reframes: "I understand. Time will pass either way—and in 6 months you'll either have your TEDx talk live or you'll still be thinking about it. I'm sorry I couldn't help you choose the first option." → End warmly

😰 FEAR OBJECTION: "What If My Talk Isn't Good Enough?"

Common fear objections: "I'll freeze on stage" / "No one will watch" / "What if I fail?"

Step 1: Agreeance Statement

"I'd be concerned too if I were in your shoes. That's totally normal."

Step 2: Perspective or Certainty Frame

Option A: Perspective

"Here's the thing: We all make decisions based on our perspective, right? Your perspective right now is 'What if I'm not good enough?' But can I share Kevin Breel's perspective?"
Kevin was 19, living in his mom's basement, never spoke publicly before. His perspective was 'I'm going to embarrass myself.' But he trusted the process—30 rehearsals with his coach—and now he has 5M views and a $100K book deal. His perspective CHANGED after he did the work.
"What if YOUR perspective changes too once you go through our process?"

Option B: Certainty

"What you're really seeking is certainty, right? You want to KNOW for sure that this will work."
→ They agree
"But here's the truth: There's no certainty in anything except death and taxes. If you only took action when you had 100% certainty, you'd never do ANYTHING. Right?"

Step 3: TEDx-Specific Reframes

"My talk won't be good enough"

"That's exactly why we do 15-30 rehearsals with you BEFORE you go on stage. You're not winging this. Kevin Breel practiced 30 times. By the time you step on that red dot, your talk will be READY. Does that make sense?"

"I'll freeze on stage"

"Let me ask you this: What's scarier—freezing for 2 seconds on stage, or never sharing your message at all and watching it die inside you?"
→ PAUSE

"No one will watch my talk"

"We GUARANTEE 50,000 views in 90 days or we keep working until you hit it. It's literally impossible for no one to watch. We run the ads, optimize the thumbnails, amplify the reach. That's OUR job—not yours."

Step 4: Consequence → Reclose

"So let me ask you: If you let this fear win and you don't give your TEDx talk, what happens to your message?"
→ Let them feel it
"Are you willing to let fear make that decision for you?"
→ They say NO
"Then let's not. Let's get you started with your coach this week and trust the process that's already worked for Kevin, Eli, Susan, and hundreds of others."
→ RECLOSE
⚠️ Maximum 4 Reframes: If they're still stuck after 4 different fear reframes, it's not about fear—it's about commitment. Apologize and move on.
