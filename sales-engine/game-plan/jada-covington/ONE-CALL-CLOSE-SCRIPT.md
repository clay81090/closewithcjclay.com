# Jada Covington — One-Call-Close Script (Word-for-Word)

**Use this file on the call.** Full snapshot and brief are in the sibling files in this folder.

| Field | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Lead type** | Marketing lead (no setter handoff on file) |
| **Occupation** | Healthcare professional |
| **Stated pain** | Income issues |
| **Stated goal** | Further career in sales |
| **Self-reported strengths** | Hard-working, ready to learn, 110% dedicated, great at connecting with people |
| **Default close path** | **Setter** ($3k) if income pressure is high · **Closer** ($6k) if income goals and bandwidth support it |
| **Buyer rating** | **6.8 / 10** — strong attitude, thin data, income stress = payment sensitivity risk |

**Tone:** Warm, steady, belief-building. She is not a burned-out executive — she is a capable healthcare worker who needs a **real plan**, not hype.

---

## 1. Opening / Frame (2 min)

**CJ:** Jada, hey — this is CJ Clay with High Ticket Sales Academy. How are you doing?

*(Let her answer.)*

**CJ:** Good, good. I appreciate you taking the time to hop on. I know you came in through one of our ads or one of our pages, so you may not have spoken to anyone on our team yet — this might be the first real conversation. Is that right?

*(Confirm.)*

**CJ:** Perfect. Here's how I'd like to use our time, if that's cool with you.

**CJ:** I want to understand what's actually going on in your world right now — what you're dealing with, what you're trying to change, and what you're actually trying to build.

**CJ:** From there, if I think what we do is a strong fit, I'll walk you through exactly how it works, what the path into sales looks like, and what it would take to get started.

**CJ:** And if it's **not** a fit, I'll tell you that too. I'd rather have an honest conversation than waste your time. Fair?

*(Let her agree.)*

**CJ:** Awesome. Before we dive in — you're coming in as a healthcare professional, is that right?

*(Confirm.)*

**CJ:** Good. And just so I know how to coach you best — are you in a place where you can talk openly for the next little bit? Not rushing to a shift, not multitasking?

*(Confirm she's present.)*

## 2. Situation Audit — Current Reality (5–7 min)

**CJ:** So tell me — what does life look like right now? Walk me through the healthcare side. What do you do, and what does a typical week feel like for you?

*(Let her talk. Listen for: role, hours, stress, pay structure, burnout, schedule rigidity.)*

**CJ:** How long have you been in healthcare?

*(Note answer.)*

**CJ:** And when you filled out the form, you mentioned you're having **income issues**. I want to understand that the right way — not in a judgmental way, just so I know what we're actually solving.

**CJ:** What does that look like for you month to month? Is it that the job isn't paying enough? Hours got cut? Bills went up? Something changed recently?

*(Let her explain. Do not interrupt. Mirror key words.)*

**CJ:** So if I'm hearing you right — *(repeat her situation in your own words)* — is that fair?

*(Get confirmation.)*

**CJ:** And outside of the money side — how do you **feel** about the work itself? Do you still like helping people in healthcare, or is it more that the income isn't matching the effort anymore?

*(Let her answer.)*

**CJ:** What made you start looking at **sales** specifically? That's a pretty big lane change from healthcare. What drew you to it?

*(Listen for: income ceiling, flexibility, remote, saw someone win, tired of physical/emotional drain, wants control.)*

**CJ:** Had you heard of **high-ticket sales** before you found us, or is this newer territory for you?

*(Gauge familiarity.)*

**CJ:** And be honest with me — is this more of a **"I need something to change soon"** situation, or more of a **"I'm exploring and trying to figure out my options"** situation?

*(Note urgency level.)*

## 3. Clarify the Real Pain — Income + Identity (3–4 min)

**CJ:** Can I share what I'm hearing so far?

**CJ:** It sounds like you're not lazy. It sounds like you're not afraid of hard work — you're in healthcare, you show up, you care about people.

**CJ:** But the income side isn't giving you what you need right now. And you're at a point where staying on the same path feels harder than actually making a move into something new.

**CJ:** Is that close?

*(Let her confirm or correct.)*

**CJ:** And when you say you want to **further your career in sales** — what does winning actually look like for you?

**CJ:** Is it a number? Is it freedom? Is it working from home? Is it not being on your feet twelve hours a day? What matters most?

*(Let her define heaven.)*

**CJ:** If we fast-forward **twelve months** from now and this went really well — what changed? What does your life look like?

*(Let her build the future.)*

**CJ:** And what's the monthly income number that would make you feel like, **"Okay — I'm stable, I'm breathing, I'm not stressed every time a bill hits"**?

*(Get a number. Write it down.)*

**CJ:** Is that before taxes or take-home?

*(Clarify.)*

**CJ:** And what are you bringing home right now, roughly?

*(Get current baseline — needed for price bridge later.)*

## 4. Sales Readiness — People Skills vs. Sales Experience (3 min)

**CJ:** You also wrote something that stood out to me — you said you're **hard-working**, you're **ready to learn**, you're **110% dedicated**, and you're **really good at connecting with people**.

**CJ:** That matters in sales more than most people realize.

**CJ:** Have you ever done anything sales-related before — even informally? Retail, front desk, admissions, patient coordination, anything where you had to guide someone to a decision?

*(Note experience level.)*

**CJ:** How do you feel on the phone or on Zoom with someone you don't know — natural, nervous, depends on the day?

*(Gauge confidence.)*

**CJ:** And be honest — is there any part of you that's thinking, **"I'm a healthcare person, not a salesperson"**?

*(If yes — pre-handle imposter syndrome now.)*

**If she says yes or hesitates:**

**CJ:** That's more common than you think. And here's the truth — the people who do well in high-ticket aren't always the loudest or the pushiest.

**CJ:** A lot of them are people who are **good listeners**, **good at reading people**, and **good at building trust** — which is literally what you do in healthcare every day.

**CJ:** Sales, done right, is not tricking people. It's helping the right person make the right decision. That's a transferrable skill.

*(Let her answer.)*

*(Let her feel it — don't overdramatize.)*

**CJ:** I'm not trying to scare you. I just want to understand whether this is a **real priority** for you or something you're casually curious about.

*(Listen.)*

**CJ:** Because the people who win in this industry aren't always the most talented on day one. They're the ones who are **done tolerating** a situation that isn't working and are willing to follow a real plan.

**CJ:** Does that sound like you?

*(Get buy-in.)*

## 6. Vehicle Frame — Why HTSA / High-Ticket (3 min)

**CJ:** So let me tell you why people like you end up looking at **high-ticket sales** instead of random work-from-home jobs or another healthcare gig.

**CJ:** In high-ticket, you're not grinding for minimum wage commissions on small transactions.

**CJ:** You're talking to people who already raised their hand. They already want help. They already have budget. Your job is to consult them through the decision — not chase cold leads all day.

**CJ:** And because the transactions are larger, the income per conversation is completely different from what most people picture when they hear the word "sales."

**CJ:** The reason they come to us is not just "teach me sales theory." It's:

**CJ:** **Training** — so you're not guessing.

**CJ:** **Certification** — so companies take you seriously.

**CJ:** **Placement** — so you're not applying into the void for six months.

**CJ:** And **support** — so you're not doing this alone.

**CJ:** Does that structure make sense — why someone would want a real academy instead of just watching YouTube videos and hoping?

*(Get agreement.)*

## 7. Path Decision — Setter vs. Closer (2 min) — *Internal branch; say one path out loud*

**Use this silently first, then commit on the call:**

| Signal | Recommend |
|--------|-----------|
| Income stress high, needs faster/cheaper entry, wants remote SDR/setter path first | **Setter — $3,000** |
| Income goal $8k–$15k+/mo, strong commitment, wants top earning ceiling | **Closer — $6,000** |
| Unsure | Ask: *"Are you trying to get into sales and start earning as fast and safely as possible, or are you trying to go straight to the highest-income closer roles?"* |

**CJ (if Setter path):** Based on what you've told me, I actually think the **Setter** path may be the smarter first move for you.

**CJ:** Setters book qualified appointments for closers. It's how most people enter high-ticket. The training is faster, the investment is lower, and you can be in a paid remote role sooner — which matters when income is tight right now.

**CJ (if Closer path):** Based on what you've told me, I think you're actually a strong candidate for the **Closer** path.

**CJ:** Closers take the qualified appointments, run the consultation, and close. It's higher income, higher responsibility, and a bigger investment — but if your goal is to really change your financial life, not just patch it, this is the lane.

*(Pick one and stay on it for the rest of the call unless she pushes back.)*

## 14. Objection Handling — Word for Word

### "I don't have the money right now."

**CJ:** I hear you — and I wouldn't respect you if you ignored your finances.

**CJ:** Can I ask — is it that the money literally isn't there today, or that it's scary to commit when income is already tight?

*(If scary but possible → payment plan close.)*

**CJ:** If it's scary but possible, the payment plan exists for exactly that reason — **$1,050** or **$1,750** today, not the full amount.

**CJ:** And if the money truly isn't there, I'd rather be honest: this path may not be today. But I'd rather you know the real path than pretend a $500 course will get you placed in high-ticket.

### "I need to think about it."

**CJ:** Totally fair. What specifically are you thinking through — the **money**, whether this will **actually work for someone from healthcare**, or the **timing**?

*(Isolate.)*

**CJ:** If we cleared that one thing up, would anything else be in the way?

### "I've never done sales."

**CJ:** Most of our strongest people didn't come from sales.

**CJ:** They came from nursing, teaching, customer service, hospitality — fields where they already knew how to connect, listen, and handle pressure.

**CJ:** That's you. The mechanics we teach. The heart you already have.

### "What if I fail?"

**CJ:** Fair question. Here's the honest answer: people fail when they don't follow the plan — they skip training, they skip practice, they don't show up to coaching, they don't apply.

**CJ:** People who do the work, practice the calls, and stay coachable — those are the ones who get placed.

**CJ:** You told me you're 110% dedicated. My job is to give you the plan. Your job is to run it. Can you do that?

### "How do I know this isn't a scam?"

**CJ:** You should be careful — there is a lot of garbage online.

**CJ:** Here's what I'd look at: real training, real certification, real coaching, real placement support, real community, real reviews.

**CJ:** We have **4.9 stars on Trustpilot**, hundreds of members in the Mastermind, and I can show you member stories and exactly what's included before you pay a dime.

**CJ:** I'm not asking you to trust a vibe. I'm asking you to look at the structure and decide if the path makes sense.

### "Can I start later?"

**CJ:** You can. I'd just ask — what changes between now and later?

**CJ:** Because the income pressure you described doesn't usually fix itself by waiting. If anything, the cost of waiting is another few months in the same situation.

**CJ:** If you're in, let's build the plan. If you're not, that's okay too — I'd rather know today.

### "I need to talk to my spouse / family."

**CJ:** Respect that completely. What do you think their biggest concern will be — the money or whether this is legitimate?

**CJ:** Would it help if I sent you the enrollment page so they can see exactly what's included while you're talking it through?

*(If yes → send link, book follow-up. If stalling → isolate real objection.)*
