Section 2 · 5–7 min
Decision frame — speak it like a story
This is the main frame. Slow down on the parenthetical; let them feel the two paths (fit vs not fit).
Why
Without this they think it’s an info call. You’re not trapping them — you’re naming that you might not be for everyone, and that you’ll still help either way.
"[Name], just so you know what this call is about (pause): we're going to see if High Ticket Sales makes sense for you and if you're a good fit to work with our team."

"The same way you're looking for a company that will do what they say they're going to do… we're looking for the same thing — members who are coachable and who will actually show up and put in the work."

"I'm not here to hype you up. I'm going to ask a few questions to understand your background, your current situation, and your future goals."

"If I don't think we're a good fit… I'll shoot you straight and will still do my best to help and point you in the right direction."

"If we are a good fit… I'll walk you through exactly how we train you, certify you, and place you into an offer — and we'll get you started by the end of the call."
"Sound fair?" Stop talking. Let them answer.
