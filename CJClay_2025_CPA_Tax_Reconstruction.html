Narrative timeline and business context

Feb 4
Brent asked CJ to become a 50/50 partner
Brent was to hold 51% because he had the license and LLC. CJ was to hold 49%. No operating agreement was ever completed. CJ then dropped everything and began building the company with the expectation that the ownership would be finalized.
Feb–Jul
CJ built sales and revenue side of 1845 Roofing
CJ handled sales, marketing, CRM, lead generation, contractor relationships, and front-end revenue creation. Brent handled production and labor. During this phase Brent lived at CJ’s house and did not pay what CJ requested. Payments to CJ were random and often sent by Zelle to the personal account instead of the LLC.
Mar 2025
Tax adviser told CJ not to put himself in as shareholder yet
The advice was to avoid naming CJ as shareholder because Brent had prior unpaid taxes. CJ followed that advice, expecting Brent would clean it up as revenue increased. That never happened.
May 30
First W-2 payroll starts through FrankCrum
CJ and Brent gave themselves equal reasonable salary treatment through FrankCrum. Payroll began late, and CJ reports that two payments were later withheld illegally. CJ also states no 941 work was completed for 2025.
Jul 23
Falling out after CJ had worked on a $2.5M project
CJ had worked for months on a roughly $2.5M project. In July the relationship with Brent broke down and CJ left. He had no lawyer, no completed partnership protection, and no continued income from HD Roofing.
Aug 18
Prior adviser / POA dropped CJ midstream
CJ received a letter saying the adviser was no longer representing him and was only given a vague list of next steps. That left CJ without payroll support, filing help, or transition support during an already unstable period.
Sep–Oct
Short return to roofing, then pivot
CJ went back to HD Roofing briefly, then Apex for about three weeks, then later moved into remote high-ticket sales and got back to $10k+/month after several months.

What a CPA needs to understand from this story

  • Income did not flow cleanly through one entity or one account.
  • Some payments that should have gone to the LLC were paid personally.
  • Payroll started late and was not fully maintained.
  • The prior adviser leaving in August is part of the explanation for the compliance failure.
